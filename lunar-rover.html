Lunar Rover Project

Mission Concept Academy team project focused on designing a rover for lunar ice exploration in permanently shadowed regions.

Overview

This project was completed through the Mission Concept Academy, where student teams were assigned a NASA-style mission with technical deliverables, role assignments, and real design constraints.

My Role

I was selected as CAD lead and was responsible for building and refining the 3D model of the rover based on real component dimensions. After our lead electrical engineer left the program, I also stepped into the electrical lead role and helped determine power needs, system requirements, and design feasibility.

Challenge

Our mission was to design a rover capable of traversing the Moon in search of lunar ice in permanently shadowed regions, while staying within mass, power, and budget limitations and accounting for the long lunar night when sunlight was unavailable.

Outcome

Taking on both CAD and electrical responsibilities pushed me to think about the rover as a single connected system. Our team was among the top teams to successfully complete the mission.
